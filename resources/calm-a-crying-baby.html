Calm a Crying Baby: 7 Steps

Not medical advice. For emergencies, call your local emergency number.

  1. Safety scan: Check breathing, color, temperature, and surroundings; address any urgent issues first.
  2. Basic needs: Offer a feed if due, burp, check diaper, and adjust clothing or room temperature.
  3. Comfort measures: Hold close or skin‑to‑skin, rock gently, add white noise or shushing.
  4. Gas relief: Bicycle legs, gentle tummy massage (clockwise), and keep upright after feeds.
  5. Swaddle safely: Hip‑healthy wrap, back‑sleeping only, stop swaddling at first signs of rolling.
  6. Environment: Lower stimulation—dim lights, reduce noise, use consistent bedtime cues.
  7. Red flags: Fever, lethargy, inconsolable high‑pitched cry, fewer wet diapers—seek professional care.
